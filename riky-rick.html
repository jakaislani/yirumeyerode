Riky Rick Age & Early Life

Riky was born on July 20, 1987, in KwaMashu, Natal, South Africa. While finishing his education at KwaMashu High School, he became immersed in music and singing. He studied at Hilton College when he decided to pursue his rapping and singing profession full-time. On February 10, 2022, he was admitted to South Africa’s most prestigious university, the University of Cottonfest. He got accepted to this university to finish his bachelor’s degree in Cottonology and Reaganomics. He has resumed his studies since it was his mother’s wish that he complete his education.

Career

He began struggling at an early age and began studying music and musical instruments, as well as creating raps. His first studio session was with Bongani Fassie. Throughout his life, Bongani Fassie was a genuine inspiration to him. Riky was inspired by Fassie to write his songs. He then released his debut track, “Barbershop,” which he co-wrote with South African rapper Da L.E.S. His popular tune “Nafukwas” helped him gain international notoriety. He didn’t stop there, and throughout his career, he published several songs back to back.

In 2015, he released his album Family Values. This album cover has a photograph of his boyhood home in KwaMashu. In 2017, he released two Extended Plays (E.P.s) named Scooby Snacks E.P. and Stay Shining E.P. Riky, who is well-versed in fashion and music, began his Cotton Fest tradition in 2018. Cotton Fest is reported to be a once-a-year party in which he invites several well-known celebrities, superstars, and musicians to one location. He also began selling Cotton Fest-branded items. Riky owned a record label and two firms, Makhado Makhado Agency and Cotton Club Records. He won the MTV Africa Music Award in 2015 for his video single Nafukwa.
