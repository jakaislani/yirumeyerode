7 Best Wing Sauces to Buy

The best wing sauce is flavorful, affordable, and has a great cling factor. We gathered some of the most popular wing sauces at the grocery store and tasted them all to find the best Buffalo wing sauce, the best hot wing sauce, the best garlic parmesan wing sauce, and more.

What is wing sauce you might ask? We consider wing sauce to be any sauce that is intended to be slathered on fried chicken wings—most of them have “wing sauce” written on the label but we didn’t only taste wing sauces that declared themselves to be wing sauce and only wing sauce. Buffalo sauce is obviously the most common wing sauce—and it’s probably the best wing sauce. But it’s not the only wing sauce out there. Over the course of two separate taste tests, we tried a world of wing sauces, including garlic parm, soy-flavored, BBQ, and hot wing sauce.

Some cost an arm and a leg. Others flew under the flavor radar. A couple couldn’t drum up any excitement at all. The best wing sauces had us flocking to them.

Okay, now that we got that out of our system, let’s talk about what we look for in the best chicken wing sauce. We want sauce that sticks to a wing—it can make a mess of your fingers but it shouldn’t all drip off the skin and pool under the wing. We want vibrant flavor. No matter what type of wing sauce, it should be powerful enough to stand up to fatty chicken skin and juicy meat. And the best wing sauce should keep you coming back for more. Sure, the chicken (or the cauliflower or the chicken substitute) is great, but in reality, wings are really about the sauce. That’s why you end up eating a dozen of the dang things. 

Whether you’re cooking for game day or just putting together your own appetizer sampler for a solo meal, here’s our updated ranking of the best wing sauce to buy—now with a new number one!

Best Tangy

Sweet Baby Ray’s Buffalo Wing SauceTarget

If you like your Buffalo sauce heavy on the vinegar—and I mean heavy—then this mouth-puckering condiment from Sweet Baby Ray’s is the best Buffalo wing sauce for you. It’s creamy and tomato-heavy, but mostly it’s a tang fest. And though there is a touch of heat, which builds as you eat, it’s also some of the best mild wing sauce we tasted. If you can’t handle any heat at all, I think you’re just going to have to opt for an Asian-style wing sauce or garlic parm. But if you can manage a touch of a tingle, then this mild Buffalo wing sauce will treat you right.  —Justine Sterling

Best Garlic Parmesan

Buffalo Wild Wings Garlic Parmesan Wing SauceWalmart

Buffalo Wild Wings almost had a clean sweep of this list. They make good wing sauces! And one of Buffalo Wild Wings’ best sauce varieties is the garlic parm. It’s super herby and has big chunks of garlic and cheese. It’s buttery in a real-dairy way and doesn’t have an artificial or oily taste. If you love wings with lots of flavor but zero spice, this is the best garlic parmesan wing sauce to buy. —Jordan Myrick

Best Fruity

Buffalo Wild Wings Mango Habanero Wing SauceWalmart

Buffalo Wild Wings Mango Habanero Wing Sauce is intense. If you’re not a fan of flavor, you won’t be a fan of this. Flavor lovers, on the other hand, will love this sweet and spicy sauce. It should actually be described as spicy and sweet because this sauce is hot. The texture is sticky and the flavor is fruity. It’s also super acidic. This sauce is a nice change of pace from other wing sauces and is the best chicken wing sauce for anyone feeling a little adventurous. —Jordan Myrick

Best Hot Wing Sauce

Melinda’s Creamy Style Ghost Pepper Wing SauceTarget

If you’re looking for a spicy wing sauce that gives intense heat and flavor, this is the best hot wing sauce out there. Melinda’s Creamy Style Ghost Pepper Wing Sauce is actually spicy! You have to have some serious heat tolerance to handle it. Luckily, I do and I love it. But don’t worry, it’s not just heat. It has a complex taste with notes of garlic and vinegar. I brought this bottle home and I’ve been putting it on basically everything. —Jordan Myrick

Best Soy

Buffalo Wild Wings Asian Zing SauceTarget

Buffalo Wild Wings Asian Zing is the best “Asian” sauce we tried. I do, however, have some issues with this name and this category of wing in general. What do they mean by “Asian?!” Asia is absolutely gigantic, and cuisines, ingredients, and flavors can vary from not only country to country, but region to region, city to city. That’s why I am naming this wing sauce the best chicken wing sauce with a soy sauce base. And as much as I hate the lazy name, I can’t deny that it tastes very, very good. —Jordan Myrick

Best Spicy Buffalo

Frank’s Red Hot Buffalo Wing SauceTarget

Hot sauce enthusiasts were surprised that Frank’s Original RedHot didn’t make its way onto our ranking of the best hot sauces. That’s because the real star in the Frank’s family is Frank’s Wing Sauce. And Frank’s RedHot Buffalo Wing Sauce is some of the best Buffalo sauce for wings. In fact, in our original ranking, it was our number one pick for the best store bought wing sauce. And though it was unseated, it’s still the best Buffalo wing sauce out there. It’s so rich and buttery, and perfectly spiced. If you’re looking for a classic, spicy Buffalo wing, grab a bottle of Frank’s original Buffalo wing sauce (make sure it’s the wing sauce, specifically!). —Jordan Myrick

Best of the Bests

Frank’s RedHot Wings Mild Wings SauceWalmart

After our latest wing sauce taste test, a new Frank’s RedHot wing sauce took over the top spot from the Buffalo variety. We never expected a mild wing sauce to be so incredible, but it is everything we ever wanted in the best wing sauce. It’s so extremely creamy—it’s decadent. But it’s also quite tangy, so you’re getting that balance of acidity and fat in every bite. Plus, even though it’s categorized as mild (one step below Buffalo on the Frank’s scale), it still has some heat. Even heat-seeker Jordan Myrick agreed that this mild Frank’s beat out Frank’s Buffalo wing sauce. Trust us, this is the best chicken wing sauce. —Justine Sterling
