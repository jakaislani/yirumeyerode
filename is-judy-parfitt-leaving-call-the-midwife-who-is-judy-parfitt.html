Is Judy Parfitt Leaving Call the Midwife?

Contrary to rumors, actress Judy Parfitt is not leaving “Call the Midwife” and continues to be an integral part of the beloved show, much to the delight of viewers. Portraying the role of Sister Monica Joan, an elderly nun with early-stage dementia, Judy Parfitt brings a unique perspective to the BBC series with her strong educational background and knowledge of classical literature.

Fans can look forward to enjoying Judy Parfitt’s portrayal in “Call the Midwife,” as she remains a vital member of the cast.

Who is Judy Parfitt?

Born in Sheffield, Yorkshire, England, Judy Parfitt is a distinguished English actress with a career spanning over six decades. Her journey in the entertainment industry began in the 1950s, and she gained recognition for her roles in various productions, including the BBC television serial “David Copperfield” (1966) and Tony Richardson’s film adaptation of “Hamlet” (1969).

Notably, Parfitt earned accolades for her role as Mildred Layton in the 1984 ITV series “The Jewel in the Crown” and for portraying Lady Catherine de Bourgh in the 1980 TV version of “Pride and Prejudice.” Her diverse career also includes notable performances in films such as “Dolores Claiborne” (1995) and “Girl with a Pearl Earring” (2003). Since 2012, she has been a significant cast member of the popular drama series “Call the Midwife,” where she portrays Sister Monica Joan.

Judy Parfitt’s early life includes attending Notre Dame High School for Girls and graduating from the Royal Academy of Dramatic Art in 1953. Throughout her career, she has remained an esteemed figure in the entertainment industry, known for her talent and contributions over the years.

Call the Midwife

“Call the Midwife” is a beloved British television series set in the late 1950s and 1960s, depicting the lives of nurse midwives in London’s East End.
